# OpenAI Audition Strategy

## One-Line Strategy

Use Codex to create a public proof-of-work application that demonstrates what Codex is becoming.

## Audience

Primary:

- OpenAI product and engineering leaders.
- Codex team members.
- Builders on X who can amplify the story.

Secondary:

- Founders, engineers, and operators interested in proof-of-work hiring.

## What They Should Think

After seeing the project, the ideal reaction is:

> This person understands the product, can create a sharp public narrative, and can ship with agents without losing judgment.

## Positioning

The campaign is not:

- "AI wrote my resume."
- "I built a random app with Codex."
- "Please hire me."

The campaign is:

> A public demonstration of Codex turning ambition into execution.

## Launch Narrative

1. Noah asks Codex how to get hired by OpenAI.
2. Codex recommends a public proof-of-work audition.
3. The repo records strategy, scorecards, prompts, rejected ideas, artifacts, and receipts.
4. The application becomes the shipped body of work.

## Success Signal

Minimum success:

- Builders understand and share the premise.

Strong success:

- OpenAI employees engage with the artifact.

Target success:

- The project starts a real hiring conversation.
