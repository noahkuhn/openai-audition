# Mission

## Objective

Get Noah Kuhn hired by OpenAI through public proof of work generated with Codex.

## Campaign Title

**I Asked Codex To Get Me Hired At OpenAI**

## Thesis

Codex should not be framed as autocomplete or a faster ticket machine.

The stronger claim:

> Codex can help a human turn intent into durable public work.

This repo is the test case.

## What Counts As Winning

Primary win:

- Someone at OpenAI with hiring influence notices the project and takes Noah seriously enough to start a real conversation.

Secondary wins:

- Builders share the project because the premise is clean and the artifact is useful.
- Codex team members recognize their product direction in the workflow.
- The public record demonstrates taste, judgment, execution, and supervision of agents.
- The process becomes reusable for other people pursuing proof-of-work applications.

## The Meta Angle

The pitch is valuable because it is recursive:

1. The user asks Codex how to get hired by OpenAI.
2. Codex chooses a public proof-of-work strategy.
3. Codex helps create the artifacts, site, process, launch plan, and final pitch.
4. The resulting artifact demonstrates what Codex makes possible.

The story OpenAI would want to be true:

> A person used Codex to convert ambition into execution.

## What This Is Not

- Not a fake autonomous startup.
- Not a resume dressed up as a repo.
- Not a generic wrapper around an LLM.
- Not a deep SaaS build unless the scorecard proves that is necessary.
- Not a crypto project by default.
- Not an attempt to impersonate OpenAI endorsement.

## Non-Negotiables

- Be honest about what Codex did and what Noah did.
- Publish failures, rejected ideas, and dead ends.
- Avoid fake metrics.
- Keep claims verifiable.
- Keep the first public version light enough to ship fast.
- Make the process legible before adding product depth.

## Current Bet

The artifact itself is the product:

> A public audition system where Codex plans, scores, builds, documents, and launches a proof-of-work application.

The OpenAI audition is the first case study.
