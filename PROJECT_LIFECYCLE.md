# Project Lifecycle

This project has four phases.

Each phase has a different job and a different artifact standard.

## Phase 1: Unknown

State:

- OpenAI knows nothing about Noah.
- Nobody has a reason to care yet.

Goal:

- Create a premise that is easy to repeat.

Primary artifact:

- `index.html`
- `launch/x-thread-draft.md`
- `launch/video-outline.md`

Standard:

- Interesting before explanation.
- Legible to a stranger in one sentence.
- No raw process dump.

## Phase 2: Viral Attention

State:

- People see the hook on X, Hacker News, GitHub, or through shares.

Goal:

- Make them click through and understand the bet.

Primary artifact:

- Landing page.
- Short video.
- X thread.
- Repo README.

Standard:

- The story must fit in a screenshot.
- The first 10 seconds matter more than the full archive.
- The repo must not look like a pile of notes.

## Phase 3: OpenAI Due Diligence

State:

- Someone at OpenAI or adjacent to OpenAI is curious enough to inspect.

Goal:

- Show judgment, taste, execution, and honest human-agent collaboration.

Primary artifact:

- `CASE_FILE.md`
- `APPLICATION.md`
- `ARTIFACT_LEDGER.md`
- `RECEIPTS.md`
- selected files under `evidence/`

Standard:

- Clean narrative path.
- Specific receipts.
- Clear distinction between Codex work and human judgment.
- No noise.

## Phase 4: Hiring Conversation

State:

- The project has started a real conversation.

Goal:

- Convert attention into a serious evaluation of fit.

Primary artifact:

- `APPLICATION.md`
- `auditions/openai/postmortem.md`
- strongest evidence links.

Standard:

- No hype.
- Explain what was learned.
- Explain what would be valuable inside OpenAI.
- Show where the project failed or needed human correction.

## Storage Implication

Raw conversations are not the public artifact.

They are source material used to produce:

- case file entries,
- receipts,
- selected transcript excerpts,
- launch clips,
- build-log updates.
