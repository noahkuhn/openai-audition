# Project Scorecard

Candidate projects are scored against the hiring objective, not against abstract startup potential.

Scale: 1 to 5.

## Criteria

### 1. OpenAI Narrative Fit

Does this make Codex look like the future OpenAI is trying to build?

High score signals:

- Long-running work.
- Tool use.
- Memory.
- Human approval.
- Multi-step execution.
- Clear before/after leverage for the human.

### 2. Viral Clarity

Can a normal person understand and repeat the idea in one sentence?

High score signals:

- The hook is obvious.
- The stakes are human.
- The premise creates curiosity before details.
- The artifact has a clean screenshot or video opening.

### 3. Technical Credibility

Would serious builders see real work instead of a wrapper?

High score signals:

- Public repo.
- Real artifacts.
- Non-trivial workflow.
- Failure modes visible.
- Verification or receipts.

### 4. Ship Speed

Can the first public version ship in days, not months?

High score signals:

- Static site or lightweight app first.
- Existing context can be reused.
- No dependency on external approvals.
- No legal or privacy bottleneck.

### 5. Proof Density

Does the project produce receipts naturally?

High score signals:

- Logs.
- Diffs.
- Prompts.
- Screenshots.
- Decisions.
- Public artifacts.
- Before/after evidence.

### 6. Hiring Signal

Does this demonstrate taste, judgment, engineering ability, and agency?

High score signals:

- It shows what to build and what not to build.
- It shows supervision of agents, not blind delegation.
- It demonstrates communication and product sense.
- It looks like work someone at OpenAI would want on the team.

### 7. Repeatability

Could the process generalize beyond Noah and OpenAI?

High score signals:

- Another person can reuse the method.
- The system can target another company or role.
- The artifact becomes a new primitive for proof-of-work applications.

## Current Bias

The best candidate is not necessarily the deepest build.

For this campaign, the winning project must make the process visible:

> Codex receives an ambition, creates a plan, supervises execution, produces public evidence, and turns the result into an application.
